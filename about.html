About Hackegerton

A university hackathon bringing together talented students to innovate, collaborate, and build solutions that make a difference. Starting January 12 online, culminating in an in-person meet on February 28.

Why Hackegerton?

01

What is Hackegerton?

Hackegerton is a university hackathon designed to bring together talented students to build innovative solutions, network with peers, and compete for exciting prizes. Starting January 12 online and culminating in an in-person meet on February 28.

02

Our Mission

To provide a platform where students can showcase their creativity, learn new technologies, collaborate with peers, and solve real-world problems through innovation and teamwork.

03

Who Can Participate?

Hackegerton is open to all university students. You can participate individually or form teams. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced developer, there's a place for you at Hackegerton.

04

What to Expect

Expect workshops, mentorship sessions, networking opportunities, and the chance to build something amazing. Compete across multiple tracks including AI, Blockchain, Web/Mobile, and more.

Our Story

The Beginning

Hackegerton was created to bring together the brightest minds from universities to collaborate, innovate, and build solutions that matter. We believe in the power of student innovation and creativity.

The Experience

Starting online on January 12, participants have the flexibility to work remotely while building their projects. The journey culminates in an exciting in-person event on February 28 where teams present their innovations.

The Impact

Through Hackegerton, we've seen incredible projects come to life, lasting friendships formed, and careers launched. Join us in creating the next generation of tech innovators.

Organizing Team

Organized By

Egerton University Computer Science Students Association (EUCOSSA)

The Egerton University Computer Science Students Association (EUCOSSA) is a vibrant student organization dedicated to fostering excellence in computer science education and innovation at Egerton University. EUCOSSA serves as a platform for computer science students to connect, learn, and grow together through various academic, technical, and social activities.

The association organizes workshops, hackathons, coding competitions, and networking events that help students develop their technical skills, stay updated with industry trends, and build meaningful connections with peers and professionals. EUCOSSA is committed to empowering the next generation of computer scientists and technologists by providing opportunities for hands-on learning, collaboration, and innovation.

Hackegerton 2026 is proudly organized by the EUCOSSA 2025/2026 Executive Team, a dedicated group of student leaders committed to creating an exceptional hackathon experience that brings together talented students from across the university and beyond.

Visit eucossa.com to learn more about EUCOSSA and stay updated with our activities and events.

Collaborators

Innovation Hub Egerton [ihub]

Innovation Hub Egerton (ihub) is a dynamic innovation center that supports entrepreneurship, technology development, and creative problem-solving within the Egerton University community. ihub provides resources, mentorship, and a collaborative environment where students can transform their ideas into viable solutions and startups.

Engineering Students Association

The Engineering Students Association at Egerton University represents and supports engineering students across all disciplines. The association promotes technical excellence, professional development, and interdisciplinary collaboration, making it an ideal partner for Hackegerton as we bring together students from various technical backgrounds to solve real-world challenges.
